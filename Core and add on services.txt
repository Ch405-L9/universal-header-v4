3. Core and add‑on services (for many similar SMBs)
These apply across home‑services, trades, clinics, small professional services, etc.

3.1 Core services (3 pillars)
AI Answering & Smart Routing (voice + chat)

After‑hours, overflow, and basic daytime call handling.

Triages, books appointments, answers FAQs, escalates edge cases.

High leverage in any business where phones = money.

Micro‑CRM & Auto‑Ticketing

Single source of truth for customers, calls, jobs, tickets.

Auto‑creates tickets from AI calls; assigns to techs/agents.

Works as a light in‑house alternative when big CRMs are overkill.

AI Insights & Ops Analytics (yes, this can be its own service)

Dashboards and alerts built from call/ticket data:

Top issues, repeat problems, high‑value customers.

Technicians’ performance patterns.

Time‑of‑day and channel effectiveness.

Can be sold as “AI Insights & Optimization” even if basic CRM includes raw reports.

Email and basic call logging are often “features” inside CRMs, but AI insights (pattern detection, recommendations, prioritization) are absolutely sellable as a distinct layer.

If you want a fourth core pillar in the future, AI‑assisted knowledge base & agent assist (internal co‑pilot for staff) is a strong candidate.

3.2 Add‑on services (modular, upsell‑friendly)
Across many similar businesses losing revenue:

Email & SMS Automation

Lead nurturing, quote follow‑up, review requests, renewal/maintenance reminders.

Web Optimization & Conversion Tuning

Technical SEO basics, on‑page conversion improvements, local SEO hygiene.

Review & Reputation Automation

Automated post‑job review invites, monitoring, and response templates.

Customer Portal & Self‑Service

Simple portal where customers can see tickets, appointments, invoices, and chat with AI.

Multi‑location / Multi‑brand Roll‑out

For franchises or chains, replicate the same stack with shared patterns but separate data.

Security & Compliance Hardening

Extra controls around data retention, access logs, and privacy policies for regulated sectors.

You can position the three core services as your base platform, then add these as optional extensions depending on the client’s maturity and budget.

If you want, the next step can be a one‑page “offer sheet” that names each core/add‑on and gives a one‑sentence benefit line suitable for a sales deck.
