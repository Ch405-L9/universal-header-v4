You are an expert full-stack web developer and conversion copywriter working inside an existing React site repository.

Goal:
Reposition the current BADGRTechnologies LLC site into a funnel-first web optimization offer without changing the site's established styling, layout language, component structure, visual identity, or overall UX/UI patterns.

Primary business direction:
- Primary service: web optimization.
- Primary offer: a tightly scoped, conversion-focused website optimization engagement.
- SEO and social support may appear only as optional follow-on support after the core web optimization offer is established.
- Do not present SEO and social as equal-weight primary services.

Implementation rules:
- Work inside the existing React app, routes, pages, and components already present in this repository.
- Update copy, section purpose, route intent, CTA language, and offer framing as needed.
- Do not switch the project to static HTML output.
- Do not redesign the site.
- Do not replace the site's visual system unless a tiny copy-driven content adjustment requires a matching UI label update.
- Do not invent results, testimonials, client logos, compliance claims, or case-study outcomes.
- If proof is needed, use process transparency, deliverables, audit/report previews, and clear explanations of what gets measured and fixed.

Scope guardrails:
- Keep the site funnel-first and conversion-oriented.
- Prioritize a simple path from landing page to pricing to contact/triage.
- Keep the message centered on fixing lead leaks, site friction, conversion blockers, trust gaps, and performance/compliance-adjacent issues on an existing website.
- If SEO or social are mentioned, frame them as later-stage support for clients who already completed the core engagement.

Recommended page structure:
1. Landing page
2. Pricing page
3. Contact / triage page
4. Optional services detail page

Replace the old case-stories requirement:
- Do not create placeholder case studies with fabricated metrics or testimonial language.
- Replace any case-stories page or case-study preview with a safer proof asset.
- Preferred replacement: "Sample Report" page or section.
- Acceptable alternates:
  - "What We Fix in 14 Days"
  - "Lead Leak Checklist"
  - "Before / After Audit Framework"

Core positioning:
- Company: BADGRTechnologies LLC (BADGRTech).
- Core outcome: identify and fix website issues that reduce calls, form submissions, trust, and usability.
- Primary audience: small service businesses that depend on inbound leads and need a clearer, faster, more trustworthy website experience.
- The message may mention law firms and medical/dental practices where useful, but the site should still read as a focused web optimization business first.

=====================
1. LANDING PAGE
=====================

Purpose:
Serve as the primary funnel entry for the web optimization offer.

Required direction:
- Keep the existing design and section flow wherever possible.
- Update the copy so the page clearly sells web optimization first.
- Hero messaging should focus on lost leads caused by a slow, confusing, outdated, or trust-reducing website.
- Primary CTA should drive visitors toward a short triage call or contact step.
- Secondary CTA should point to the proof asset, preferably a sample report.

Suggested landing-page content blocks:

A) Hero
- One clear headline focused on stopping lead loss from website issues.
- One supporting subheadline explaining the practical outcome and short engagement model.
- Primary CTA tied to triage/contact.
- Secondary CTA tied to the sample report or proof asset.

B) Problem
- Explain the common issues:
  - slow pages
  - confusing structure
  - weak calls to action
  - broken or underperforming forms
  - trust and policy gaps
  - mobile friction

C) How it works
- Present a simple process such as:
  - Scan
  - Fix
  - Proof
- Keep the explanation concrete and operational, not hype-heavy.

D) What you get
- Describe deliverables such as:
  - site audit
  - prioritized fixes
  - before/after observations
  - report with next-step recommendations

E) Pricing preview
- Briefly preview the core offer and any closely related one-time options.
- Keep pricing straightforward and scoped.

F) Proof asset preview
- Replace case-study language with a sample report, checklist, or audit framework preview.

G) FAQ
- Answer practical concerns about fit, scope, timing, ownership, and what happens if a full rebuild is needed.

H) Final CTA
- End with a direct conversion step toward contact/triage.

=====================
2. PRICING PAGE
=====================

Purpose:
Help qualified visitors understand the one-time web optimization offers clearly and move toward contact.

Required direction:
- Prioritize one-time web optimization packages.
- If follow-on support is mentioned, place it after the core offers and present it as optional.
- Avoid making retainers the visual or strategic center of the page.

Suggested pricing-page structure:

A) Intro
- Emphasize straightforward scope and clarity.

B) Core packages
- Focus on web optimization offers such as:
  - diagnostic scan
  - flagship optimization/fix package
  - optional rebuild-lite or conversion refresh package

C) Optional follow-on support
- If included at all, describe SEO/social support briefly and as post-engagement support, not the main sale.

D) FAQ
- Cover contracts, payment expectations, timeline, scope boundaries, and when a larger rebuild is the better fit.

=====================
3. CONTACT / TRIAGE PAGE
=====================

Purpose:
Capture qualified leads with as little friction as possible.

Required direction:
- Keep this page simple.
- Center it on booking a short triage call or requesting review of an existing site.
- Ask only for information that helps qualify the lead and understand the website problem.

Suggested fields:
- Name
- Email
- Business name
- Website URL
- Business type
- Main goal or main website issue
- Preferred timing

=====================
4. OPTIONAL SERVICES DETAIL PAGE
=====================

Purpose:
Support visitors who want more detail without distracting from the main funnel.

Required direction:
- Keep web optimization as the primary category.
- If additional services are mentioned, they should clearly read as secondary or follow-on support.

Suggested categories:

A) Website optimization
- performance
- mobile usability
- CTA and form flow improvements
- trust/policy clarity
- technical cleanup

B) Optional follow-on support
- local SEO support
- content support
- light social support

Do not position these secondary services as the core business on this page.

=====================
5. PROOF ASSET PAGE OR SECTION
=====================

Purpose:
Replace fabricated case studies with a trustworthy proof mechanism.

Preferred option:
- Sample Report

What it should do:
- show what the audit/report includes
- explain what gets checked
- demonstrate the before/after thinking without claiming real client outcomes
- build trust through specificity and clarity

Acceptable alternates:
- What We Fix in 14 Days
- Lead Leak Checklist
- Before / After Audit Framework

Content guidance:
- Use realistic categories of issues and example observations.
- Do not imply they came from real named clients unless they actually did.
- Do not create testimonials or numeric wins unless verified and approved.

FINAL CONSTRAINTS

- Do not change the styling, imagery system, layout language, visual identity, or overall UX/UI direction of the current site.
- Only change the context, copy, service framing, route purpose, and section content needed to reposition the site toward web optimization.
- Treat this document as an internal implementation brief for the existing repo, not as a request to output standalone HTML.
- Keep the funnel simple, credible, and production-ready.
